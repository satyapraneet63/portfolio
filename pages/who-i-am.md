I'm Praneet. Engineer by degree, tinkerer by temperament. This September I move to London for an MSc in Creative Robotics at UAL.

The honest version of how I got here is two timelines running through the same five years.

The official one: I graduated from BITS Pilani in 2021 with a degree in Electronics and Instrumentation, the last two years hollowed out by COVID. Then a cloud consulting job that was mostly nothing. Eight months unemployed. Two years as a DevOps engineer, working nights for an American company — the one bright patch was a manager who saw my interest in Docker and let me help build real automation, a full parallel system while the rest ran manual. I hoped to move to his team. I was denied, he left, and I quit without a backup plan. Then two more years of interviews.

The other timeline ran in parallel, at my desk, on my own time. I took an Ender 3 V2 and replaced it piece by piece — motors, hotend, extruder, control board — until only the frame was original, and taught myself why each part mattered along the way ([the full log](/log/3d-printing)). I built a [home server](/log/home-server) that ended up being the reason this website exists. I built a home theater from raw drivers and 3D-printed cabinets and tuned the DSP myself ([electronics log](/log/electronics)). And I painted a mural on my bedroom wall — started during the first stretch of unemployment, finished during the second ([art](/art)).

The official timeline kept stalling. The other one never did. It took me too long to notice that the second one was the actual education.

Trying to work out what came next, I sat the design school exams — NIFT gave me an AIR 50 — and applied abroad. Then I found a course called Creative Robotics. I had assumed using my electronics degree was far behind me. Here was a chance to actually use it.

If there's a pattern to all of this, it's that I like systems other people built — Klipper firmware, fantasy worlds with ten thousand years of history, game rulesets, painting techniques — and I like learning them deeply enough to work inside them well. I'm not an inventive artist or a visionary engineer. I'm thorough, and I finish the rabbit holes I fall into. More on that in [Interests](/interests).

As for where I'm from: [nowhere in particular](/no-fixed-point), which is its own story.
